CYCLE SYNCING

19–11–2025

Listening to your body is something many people struggle with, especially women. For centuries, women lived according to the rhythms of their bodies. Today, however, the pace of modern life—with its expectations of constant productivity—makes it harder to tune in.

You feel tired? You still have to get up and do what you are supposed to do. In a fast-moving environment, it is easy to lose touch with our internal signals.

How do we reconnect? By understanding our natural cycle—and working with it rather than against it. Below, we break down each phase of the menstrual cycle and offer simple nutrition support.

MENSTRUATION (Day 1–5)

This phase is about rest, release and renewal. Energy often dips, cramps may appear, and mood can shift.

To support this phase:

  • Focus on foods rich in iron, since blood loss can deplete iron stores.
  • Foods rich in magnesium help relax muscles and support mood.
  • Omega-3 fatty acids may reduce pain and inflammation.
  • Warm, nourishing meals (soups, stews, porridges) bring comfort and support digestion.

Seeds: flaxseeds and pumpkin seeds. Early studies suggest potential benefits, though evidence remains limited.

Foods: beans, leafy greens, beets, nuts, cacao, whole grains, omega-3 rich foods.

FOLLICULAR PHASE (Day 6–13)

Energy and mood often rise as oestrogen levels increase.

  • Foods rich in vitamin D, vitamin E and β-carotene support hormonal transition.
  • Fermented foods support gut health, which plays a role in hormone balance.

Seeds: flaxseeds, pumpkin seeds.

Foods: egg yolk, mushrooms, almonds, avocado, carrots, spinach, fermented foods.

OVULATION (Day 14–16)

The peak phase: high energy, sociability, and confidence.

  • Vitamin B-complex and zinc support hormonal peaks.
  • Sweet fruits provide antioxidants and gentle sugars.

Foods: leafy greens, whole grains, eggs, fish, figs, berries, coconut water.

LUTEAL PHASE (Day 17–28)

Energy may fluctuate; grounding foods help stabilise mood and digestion.

  • Complex carbohydrates stabilise blood sugar.
  • Protein supports satiety and energy.
  • Cruciferous vegetables support oestrogen metabolism.

Seeds: sesame seeds, sunflower seeds.

BOTTOM LINE

Cycle syncing invites softness and awareness into your month. Not strict rules—but gentle guidance.
