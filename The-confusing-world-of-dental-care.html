The confusing world of dental care

In a world where we are constantly bombarded by facts, half-facts, and downright myths – like “non-fluoride toothpaste is better for your teeth” or “you must rinse with mouthwash to kill bacteria” – it is no wonder we feel lost.

Your overall health is reflected by the state of your mouth. Bleeding gums are a sign of inflammation, and frequent bad breath or sensitivity can hint at deeper issues. These signs should not be ignored. (study)

Inspired by Nadine Artemis’ book Holistic Dental Care and current scientific studies, this confusing world of dental advice can be simplified into seven clear, effective steps – creating a holistic routine.

  1. OIL PULLING

    Oil pulling is an Ayurvedic practice where you swish a small amount of oil (preferably cold-pressed organic coconut oil) in your mouth for around 5-15 minutes. A meta-analysis concluded it significantly reduces total bacterial counts. Including this at the start of your morning may help with bad breath, plaque, and mild gum inflammation.

  2. SALT RINSE

    The enamel on your teeth is primarily made of hydroxyapatite, which is susceptible to acid dissolution when your mouth’s pH drops below 5.5.

    A saltwater rinse helps raise saliva pH, reducing the risk of enamel softening – especially helpful if you brush right after eating.

    Mix at home:

    • 300 mL natural spring water
    • 6 grams salt
    • 1 drop peppermint oil
    • 1 drop frankincense oil

  3. TONGUE SCRAPING

    Another practice from Ayurveda, often with a copper scraper. Randomized trials show it reduces volatile sulphur compounds (VSCs) – the main cause of bad breath – better than brushing your tongue.

  4. BRUSHING GUMS

    Brushing the gums toward the teeth stimulates blood flow and keeps gum tissue resilient. Like dry brushing skin is thought to boost circulation.

    Holistic practitioners (including Nadine Artemis) and mainstream dentists agree: brush along the gum line to disrupt plaque where it builds up first.

  5. BRUSHING TEETH

    Powered toothbrushes consistently outperform manual brushing. Studies show they remove more plaque and reduce gum inflammation.

    Unlike Nadine Artemis’ preference for non-fluoride options, decades of research prove fluoride toothpaste is the most effective way to prevent cavities.

  6. FLOSS, PICK, OR WHATEVER SUITS YOU

    Whether traditional floss, water flossers, or soft picks — what matters is cleaning between teeth daily, where most cavities and gum issues begin.

  7. SALT RINSES

    Finish with a gentle salt rinse to soothe gums, balance pH, and wash away lingering acids.

WHAT TO AVOID?

Not everything on the store shelf is a friend to your mouth.

  • Alcohol-based mouthwashes can reduce bacteria short-term, but studies show no meaningful difference vs. alcohol-free. Alcohol-free is often gentler.
  • Common toothpaste additives to consider avoiding (inspired by Nadine Artemis’ philosophy):
    • Sodium lauryl sulphate (SLS): linked to gum irritation & mouth ulcers.
    • Artificial colours, sweeteners, or preservatives: research suggests they may impact the oral or gut microbiome.
    • Triclosan: once common for plaque control, now largely phased out over safety concerns.

BOTTOM LINE

Your mouth is the gateway to your body – caring for it doesn’t have to be complicated. Combining holistic practices with solid science helps you build a ritual that feels good, nurtures your mouth, and supports lifelong health.
